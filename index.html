The Range Rover is a full-size 4x4 luxury SUV manufactured by Land Rover. It is the flagship model of the company. The production of Range Rover began in 1970, today the fourth generation cars are being produced. Land Rover has expanded its Range Rover range to include three separate models:
  • Range Rover Evoque
  • Range Rover Sport
  • Range Rover Velar

Range Rover Evoque

The Land Rover Range Rover Evoque is a premium compact SUV manufactured by Land Rover. It is a logical continuation of the Land Rover LRX concept car, which debuted in January 2008 in the city of Detroit.

The car is equipped with both 150 hp diesel engines. With. and 190 l. s., already known from the Freelander 2, and a new gasoline engine with a turbocharger and direct fuel injection, the working volume of which is 2 liters, and the power is 240 hp. With. Diesel engines can be aggregated with a 6-speed manual or 6-speed automatic transmission.

Range Rover Sport

The Land Rover Range Rover Sport is a mid-size luxury SUV built on the platform of the Land Rover Discovery 3 and produced by the British company Land Rover since 2005.

In April 2009, the 2010 Range Rover Sport was unveiled at the New York Motor Show. In 2013, a completely new Range Rover Sport was introduced in New York with 5 variations of power units under the hood and “thinner” by 420 kg due to the body, now made of high-strength aluminum alloy. The car was tested by Daniel Craig.

Range Rover Velar

The Range Rover Velar (L560) is a compact crossover SUV manufactured by the British automobile company Jaguar Land Rover. The fourth model in the Range Rover lineup, the Velar was unveiled on 1 March 2017 in London, England. Velar was released in the summer of 2017. The name "Velar" had previously been used for a series of first-generation pre-production Range Rovers in 1969. The most streamlined Range Rover in the history of the brand, drag coefficient 0.32.

Built on the Jaguar Land Rover iQ[AI] (D7a) platform, the Velar shares a number of components with the Jaguar F-Pace, XF, and XE models, notably its aluminum platform and 2874mm wheelbase. The Velar is built at the same Solihull plant, but the Range Rover is 72mm longer than the F-Pace.
